## Quality That Is Tested, Not Claimed

ISO 9001:2015 certification means our quality management system is audited and documented — not just stated on a brochure. Our in-house laboratory is the operational backbone of that system, running tests at every stage of production.

## Our Testing Stages

### Stage 1: Raw Material Inspection

Before any production begins, incoming raw materials are tested against our acceptance specifications:

- **Physical testing:** Particle size, moisture content, bulk density
- **Chemical testing:** Nutrient content (Ca, Mg, S, Zn, B, etc.), pH, organic matter percentage
- **Biological testing:** Microbial load assessment for bio-input raw materials

Materials that fail acceptance criteria are rejected and returned to the supplier. Production does not begin on a batch until raw materials are cleared.

### Stage 2: In-Process Quality Control (IPQC)

During granulation and coating, our lab team samples and tests the product at defined intervals:

- Granule size distribution (sieve analysis) checked during granulation
- Moisture content monitored through the drying phase
- Coating application rate verified during the coating run
- Visual assessment for uniformity, fines generation, and surface quality

IPQC results are logged in real time. If a parameter drifts outside specification, production is paused and the cause is identified before continuing.

### Stage 3: Finished Goods Testing

Before a batch is released for dispatch, it undergoes full finished goods analysis:

- **Physical:** Size distribution (mesh analysis), bulk density, moisture content, caking tendency
- **Chemical:** Nutrient content verification against label specification
- **Active ingredient load:** For coated products, verification that coating application achieved the target load
- **Biological:** Viability count for bio-fertilizer and bio-pesticide products

Only batches that pass all finished goods tests are cleared for dispatch.

## Batch Certificate of Analysis

Every dispatched batch is accompanied by a **Certificate of Analysis (CoA)** that includes:

- Batch number and production date
- Test results for all parameters tested
- Pass/fail against specification
- Lab analyst sign-off and date

The CoA travels with the shipment and gives you, your customers, and your regulatory team a documented record of what was tested and what the results were.

## Third-Party Lab Support

For parameters that require specialised equipment or regulatory-accepted external testing (for example, pesticide residue analysis or certain registration-related tests), we coordinate with accredited third-party laboratories and provide the client with consolidated test documentation.

## For Clients Who Want to Witness Testing

We welcome client representatives to our facility to observe testing procedures for their product batches. Scheduled witnessing visits can be arranged in advance with our quality team.
