## Precision Coating at Manufacturing Scale

Coating is where a generic granule becomes a differentiated product. The quality of the coating — its uniformity, its adhesion, its resistance to breakdown during handling — directly determines how effectively your active ingredient performs in the field.

Our coating infrastructure is designed for precision at scale: uniform coverage, high coating load capacity, and consistent batch-to-batch performance.

## Coating Capabilities

### Liquid Active Ingredient Coating
We apply bio-pesticide concentrates, liquid bio-stimulants, nutrient solutions, and other liquid actives onto granule bases using controlled spray application. The process ensures:

- Even distribution of active across the granule surface
- Controlled application rate to hit your target active ingredient load
- Adhesion integrity maintained through drying

### Powder Coating
Powdered actives — micronutrient mixes, fungicide powders, insecticide powders — are applied using a binder-assisted coating process. The granule surface is activated to accept powder adhesion, and the coating is sealed to minimise dust generation during handling.

### Multi-Layer Coating
For products requiring a combination of actives — for example, a bio-fertilizer layer plus a micronutrient layer — we can apply sequential coats with intermediate drying between layers.

### Controlled-Release Coating
Polymer or wax-based outer coatings can be applied to create a controlled-release mechanism, extending the nutrient or active ingredient release period beyond what an uncoated granule would deliver.

## Coating Load Capacity

Our granule bases are engineered to accept **over 70% coating load by weight** — significantly higher than standard bentonite-based granules, which typically max out at 30–40%.

Higher coating capacity means:

- You can achieve higher active ingredient concentrations without increasing granule size
- Fewer granules needed per hectare for the same active dose
- Better economics on high-cost active ingredients

## Bring Your Own Granules

If you already have a granule base from another manufacturer, we can evaluate it for coating compatibility. We accept client-supplied granules for coating services where the base meets our quality acceptance criteria.

## Quality Control for Coating Batches

- **Pre-coating:** Granule acceptance testing (size, moisture, surface condition)
- **In-process:** Spray rate and binder application monitoring; periodic sampling for active load verification
- **Post-coating:** Coating uniformity visual and physical check; active ingredient load analysis; caking and flowability assessment
- **Documentation:** Batch CoA with active load, moisture, and physical test results
